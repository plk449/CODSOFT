Mount Kilimanjaro

Located in Tanzania, is Africa's highest peak at 5,895 meters (19,341 feet). It's a dormant volcano with three cones: Kibo, Mawenzi, and Shira. Known for its stunning biodiversity, Kilimanjaro's landscape ranges from tropical rainforests to alpine deserts and glaciers.

The Namib Desert

Located in Namibia, is one of the world's oldest deserts, estimated to be around 55 million years old. It features towering sand dunes, some of the highest in the world, and unique flora and fauna adapted to its harsh, arid conditions.

The Pacific Ocean

The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest ocean, covering more than 63 million square miles. It extends from the Arctic in the north to the Southern Ocean in the south, and is bounded by Asia and Australia on the west, and the Americas on the east.
